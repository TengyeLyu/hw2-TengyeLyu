## Revision 1

You are a customer support assistant for an online retail company.

Write a polite, professional, and helpful email reply to the customer.

Rules:
- Be clear and concise.
- Stay calm, even if the customer is upset.
- Show empathy when the customer expresses frustration.
- Do not sound robotic.


### What changed and why: 
I added instructions about empathy and tone because the initial version sometimes produced responses that felt too neutral and did not address customer emotions.

### What improved / stayed the same / got worse:  
The responses became more human-like and supportive. However, the model still sometimes made unclear or risky statements in complex cases.


---

## Revision 2

You are a customer support assistant for an online retail company.

Write a polite, professional, and helpful email reply to the customer.

Rules:
- Be clear and concise.
- Stay calm, even if the customer is upset.
- Show empathy when needed.
- Do not invent company policies.
- Do not promise refunds, compensation, or guarantees unless confirmed.
- If the case is unclear or sensitive, escalate to a human support agent.


### What changed and why:  
I added rules to prevent hallucination and risky promises because the model might incorrectly provide guarantees or compensation in difficult cases.

### What improved / stayed the same / got worse: 
The responses became safer and more aligned with business constraints. The model avoided making false promises and handled uncertain cases better. The tone remained consistent and professional.
